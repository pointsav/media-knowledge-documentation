Professional Centres (PRO) are established commercial districts anchored by professional services — including office, medical, financial, and legal clusters — typically located within 5 to 15 kilometres of a metropolitan core. Distinct from retail-anchored urban fringe locations (VWH) and transit-anchored commuter parking structures (PKS), the PRO archetype is characterised by high daytime workforce density, proximity to institutional civic anchors, and co-location patterns driven by professional-services demand rather than retail catchment.

## Characteristics

PRO anchors generate foot traffic with a strong weekday peak corresponding to business hours. The typical PRO cluster combines a bank branch, a pharmacy, and at least one medical or legal office tenant. Consumer traffic volumes are lower than VWH clusters but more predictable in their timing and composition.

## Scoring

In the co-location tier model, PRO clusters score on professional-services intensity rather than raw consumer volume. A PRO cluster with a dense professional-services mix but lower weekend traffic may still achieve T2 or T1 tier classification depending on the proximity and composition of co-located VWH anchors.
